# Electric Thinking AI Fluency Framework
# Upload this as a Knowledge Base document in ElevenLabs

## Level 0: Foundations
Getting started with AI tools.

1. Tool Access & Activation: Have you actually opened and used an AI tool?
2. First Real Conversation: Have you had a back-and-forth, not just a one-shot query?
3. Output Judgment: Can you tell when AI output is good vs. bad?
4. Use Case Recognition: Can you identify where AI could help in your work?
5. Willingness to Iterate: Do you try again when the first response isn't right?

## Level 1: Accelerator
Using AI to speed up everyday work.

1. Context Setting: Briefing AI with role, task, and relevant inputs
2. Quick Drafting: Using AI for first drafts of written content
3. Output Editing & Direction: Redirecting AI output (tone, structure, specificity)
4. Voice-First Capture: Using voice to externalize thinking, capture recaps, dictate drafts
5. In-the-Moment Support: Reflexively reaching for AI when you hit friction

## Level 2: Thought Partner
Using AI as a collaborative thinking partner.

1. Interview Me: Letting AI lead with questions to surface your assumptions
2. Rapid Ideation: Generating multiple options before committing to one
3. Challenge Me: Asking AI to find holes, counterarguments, blind spots in your thinking
4. Decision Mapping: Structuring trade-offs, running scenarios, applying frameworks
5. Operationalize This: Converting strategy into concrete execution plans

## Level 3: Specialized Teammates
Building reusable AI tools and workflows.

1. Pattern Spotting: Recognizing when a repeating task should become a reusable tool
2. Workflow Scoping: Breaking a task into inputs, steps, and expected outputs
3. Instruction Design: Writing system prompts that produce consistent, reliable output
4. Testing & Refinement: Testing tools with real inputs and iterating through edge cases
5. Knowledge Embedding: Curating and attaching reference docs so AI has domain context

## Level 4: Agentic Workflow
Designing autonomous AI-powered systems.

1. Systems Mapping: Designing end-to-end flows, not just individual tasks
2. Automation Design: Building workflows where AI handles steps without you
3. Independent Judgment: Knowing which steps require human decision-making
4. Cross-Workflow Integration: Connecting multiple AI-powered processes together
5. Continuous Improvement: Monitoring, measuring, and refining automated systems
