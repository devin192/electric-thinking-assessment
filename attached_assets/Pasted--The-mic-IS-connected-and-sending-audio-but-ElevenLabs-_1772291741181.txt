 The mic IS connected and sending audio, but ElevenLabs is receiving it 
  garbled/distorted. This is an audio encoding mismatch. Here's the proof:

  Read the transcript. Alyssa hears SOMETHING from the user. She reports "slow
  motion," "echoing," "garbled," "heavy interference," "distorted." She can even
  partially make out words ("no rush," "yeah, that's"). This means:
  - Mic permission: working
  - WebSocket: connected and sending audio
  - Audio IS reaching ElevenLabs
  - But the audio format is wrong

  This is almost certainly a sample rate mismatch. The ElevenLabs agent is
  configured to accept PCM 16000 Hz input audio. The browser's getUserMedia captures
   audio at the device's native sample rate, usually 44100 Hz or 48000 Hz. If you're
   sending those raw samples to ElevenLabs without downsampling to 16000 Hz,
  ElevenLabs tries to play 48000 Hz audio as if it were 16000 Hz. That makes
  everything sound exactly like what Alyssa described: slow motion, distorted,
  garbled.

  The fix: You need to downsample the microphone audio from the browser's native
  sample rate (probably 48000 Hz) to 16000 Hz before sending it over the WebSocket.
  The standard approach:

  1. Create an AudioContext at the browser's native sample rate
  2. Connect the mic stream to a ScriptProcessorNode or AudioWorkletNode
  3. In the processing callback, downsample from native rate to 16000 Hz (take every
   Nth sample where N = nativeSampleRate / 16000)
  4. Convert the downsampled float32 samples to PCM Int16 (multiply by 32767 and
  clamp)
  5. Send those PCM Int16 bytes over the WebSocket

  OR: Create the AudioContext with { sampleRate: 16000 } to force the browser to
  downsample automatically. This is simpler but not supported in all browsers. Check
   if it works in Chrome first.

  Also check: Are you sending the audio as raw PCM bytes, or are you accidentally
  wrapping it in a MediaRecorder format (webm/opus)? ElevenLabs Conversational AI
  expects raw PCM Int16 Little Endian at 16000 Hz. Not webm. Not opus. Not wav with
  headers. Just raw samples.

  How to verify the fix: Have a voice conversation. When the user speaks normally,
  Alyssa should understand complete sentences and respond to the actual content. No
  "slow motion," no "garbled," no "echo." Test 3-4 turns minimum. Read the
  transcript to confirm Alyssa is responding to what was actually said.
