 The voice assessment has an audio playback bug. Do not mark this as solved until 
  you can reproduce and fix it.                                                     
   
  What's happening: When Alyssa speaks, the audio sounds garbled, overlapping, and  
  fragmented. Like multiple audio chunks are playing simultaneously instead of
  sequentially. She starts a sentence, then another sentence starts before the first
   finishes, and they pile on top of each other. The transcript looks correct (the
  text is fine), but the actual spoken audio is broken.

  This is an audio playback/buffering issue, not a conversation logic issue. The
  transcript proves the LLM responses are correct. Something in how the app receives
   and plays the audio stream from the ElevenLabs WebSocket is wrong.

  Likely causes to investigate:
  1. Audio chunks from the WebSocket are being played immediately as they arrive
  instead of being queued and played sequentially. You need an audio queue/buffer
  that plays chunks in order, waiting for each to finish before starting the next.
  2. Multiple audio elements or AudioContext sources might be getting created per
  response instead of one. Each new chunk creates a new playback instead of
  appending to the current one.
  3. The ElevenLabs WebSocket sends audio in small chunks. If your code does
  audioElement.play() on every chunk without waiting for the previous chunk to
  finish, you get exactly this overlapping garbled effect.

  The fix is an audio playback queue: Receive chunks from WebSocket, buffer them in
  order, play them sequentially (wait for one chunk's onended event before playing
  the next). OR concatenate chunks into a single audio buffer per response before
  playing.

  How to verify it's fixed: Have an actual voice conversation with the agent. Alyssa
   should speak in clear, complete sentences with no overlap or garbling. Test at
  least 3-4 back-and-forth turns.

  Do not close this until you have actually listened to the audio output and
  confirmed it sounds like a normal conversation.
